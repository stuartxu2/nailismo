# Nailismo.com Website Design Guideline

## Brand Positioning

Nailismo.com should present itself as the world's first press-on nails store for men, but the brand should not feel loud, novelty-driven, or overly aggressive. The strongest direction is Korean men's fashion editorial: clean, refined, wearable, and quietly confident.

The site should make press-on nails feel like a normal part of men's styling, similar to rings, fragrance, sneakers, watches, hair styling, or a well-chosen jacket.

Core positioning:

> Press-on nails for men. Clean sets, fast wear, built for everyday styling.

## Design Personality

The visual language should be inspired by Korean fashion retail, Seoul concept stores, K-pop stylist kits, and minimalist grooming brands.

The site should feel:

- Clean
- Editorial
- Softly masculine
- Fashion-forward
- Precise
- Wearable
- Calm, not chaotic

Avoid:

- Heavy neo-brutalism
- Loud glitch effects
- Overly bold streetwear graphics
- Harsh black-and-red dominance
- Salon-style beauty tropes
- Pastel cosmetic branding
- Cursive luxury typography
- Aggressive hype-beast language

## Color Palette

The color scheme should use traditional Japanese colors from [NIPPON COLORS](https://nipponcolors.com/). The palette should be light, muted, and editorial, with only restrained accents.

| Role | Nippon Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Main background | SHIRONERI | `#FCFAF2` | Warm editorial page background |
| Clean surface | GOFUN | `#FFFFFB` | Product cards, checkout, modal surfaces |
| Soft neutral | SHIRONEZUMI | `#BDC0BA` | Dividers, borders, disabled states |
| Primary text | KUROTSURUBAMI | `#0B1013` | Headlines, body text, high-contrast UI |
| Muted menswear blue | AINEZUMI | `#566C73` | Navigation, filters, labels |
| Fashion accent | FUJINEZUMI | `#6E75A4` | Hover states, active controls, editorial links |
| Skin-tone warmth | HAIZAKURA | `#D7C4BB` | Editorial sections, UGC tags, soft accents |
| Fresh accent | KAMENOZOKI | `#A5DEE4` | Sizing tool highlights, subtle CTA moments |

Recommended usage ratio:

- 55% SHIRONERI / GOFUN
- 25% KUROTSURUBAMI
- 10% SHIRONEZUMI
- 5% AINEZUMI
- 5% FUJINEZUMI / HAIZAKURA / KAMENOZOKI accents

The palette should not become too colorful. Accent colors should feel like styling details, not the main identity.

## Homepage Direction

The homepage should open like a Korean fashion campaign, not a conventional beauty e-commerce page.

The first viewport should show the product immediately through refined male hand styling. Use a full-width editorial image or soft-motion video featuring male hands wearing Nailismo sets with rings, knitwear, leather jackets, oversized shirts, denim, tailored coats, headphones, or clean grooming details.

Recommended hero copy:

```text
Press-On Nails for Men
Clean sets. Fast wear. Built for everyday styling.
```

Primary CTA:

```text
Shop Sets
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
Find My Size
```

Recommended homepage flow:

1. Editorial hero with male hand close-up.
2. New arrivals.
3. Best for first-time users.
4. Fit and sizing guide.
5. Lookbook-style product storytelling.
6. Short creator styling clips.
7. Reviews with real hand photos.
8. Fast checkout and shipping reassurance.

## Typography

Typography should be refined and fashion-forward.

Use:

- Slim grotesk or modern condensed sans-serif for headlines.
- Clean, readable sans-serif for body and UI text.
- Moderate type scale with strong spacing.
- Mostly sentence case or restrained uppercase.

Avoid:

- Chunky brutalist fonts.
- Graffiti or Y2K display faces.
- Overly decorative beauty typography.
- Excessively large type that makes the brand feel loud.

Example copy style:

- Clean chrome
- Soft black
- Daily matte
- Short square
- Weekend set
- Made for men's hands
- Salon finish in minutes
- Easy wear, clean removal

## Product Card Design

Product cards should be minimal, image-led, and easy to scan.

Each card should include:

- Large product image.
- Set name.
- Shape, such as Short Square, Almond, or Oval.
- Finish, such as Matte, Chrome, Sheer, or Gloss.
- Price.
- Small color or finish swatches.
- Quiet quick-add control.

Use restrained labels:

- New
- Low stock
- Editor pick
- Restocked

Avoid loud discount badges, heavy borders, or excessive promotional text.

## Product Page Design

Product pages should reduce hesitation for men who may be new to press-on nails. The page should feel calm, instructional, and stylish.

Every product page should include:

- Macro product photography.
- Male hand model photos.
- Nail length comparison.
- Nail shape comparison.
- Fit and sizing guide.
- Application time.
- Expected wear duration.
- Removal instructions.
- What is included in the kit.
- Short vertical product video.
- User-generated hand photos.
- Clear shipping, return, and payment information.

Primary CTA:

```text
Add to Bag
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
Find My Size
```

Product page messaging should focus on ease, fit, and styling:

```text
Designed for a natural men's hand fit.
Applies in minutes. Removes cleanly.
```

## Imagery And Art Direction

Photography should feel like a Korean fashion editorial.

Use:

- Soft natural light.
- Clean studio shadows.
- Neutral outfits.
- Male hand close-ups.
- Subtle grooming details.
- Rings, silver jewelry, leather, denim, wool, knits, headphones, and tailored coats.
- Cropped compositions that emphasize hand styling.

Avoid:

- Generic manicure-table imagery.
- Pink salon environments.
- Overly glossy cosmetic flat lays.
- Hyper-aggressive streetwear props.
- Visual clutter that distracts from the nails.

## Navigation And Layout

Navigation should be simple and fashion-retail oriented.

Recommended navigation:

- New
- Sets
- Best Sellers
- Fit Guide
- Lookbook
- About

Product filters should be practical:

- Length
- Shape
- Finish
- Color
- Occasion
- First-time friendly

The layout should use generous whitespace, clean grids, and soft spacing. Cards can use subtle borders or light surfaces, but they should not feel boxed-in or heavy.

## Mobile Experience

The site should be mobile-first. Most Gen Z users will arrive from TikTok, Instagram, creator links, or search on mobile.

Mobile requirements:

- Fast-loading hero media.
- Sticky bottom `Add to Bag` bar on product pages.
- One-thumb navigation.
- Swipeable product imagery.
- Vertical styling videos.
- Simple sizing flow.
- Guest checkout by default.
- Digital wallet priority.

Checkout should support:

- Apple Pay
- Google Pay
- Shop Pay
- PayPal
- Venmo
- Cash App
- Klarna, Afterpay, or Affirm

The checkout should stay under three steps wherever possible.

## Social Proof And Community

Nailismo should make the product feel easy and socially validated.

Include:

- Creator styling clips.
- Real customer hand photos.
- First-time user reviews.
- "Styled with" outfit context.
- Lookbook entries.
- Short application videos.
- Restock request list.
- Early access signup.

The community layer should feel refined, not noisy.

## Launch And Drop Strategy

Nailismo can still use limited releases, but the tone should be closer to a Korean fashion calendar than chaotic streetwear drop culture.

Recommended launch mechanics:

- Seasonal edits.
- Limited colorways.
- Early-access previews.
- Restock request pages.
- Lookbook-led launches.
- Member previews.
- Soft countdowns.

Collection name examples:

- Seoul Matte Edit
- Chrome After Dark
- Soft Armor
- Office Black
- Weekend Silver
- Quiet Chrome
- Daily Gloss

Avoid fake urgency. Scarcity should feel real, tasteful, and product-led.

## Accessibility And Performance

The site should feel elegant, but it must also be fast and usable.

Requirements:

- WCAG-compliant contrast.
- Clear focus states.
- Keyboard-accessible navigation.
- Descriptive alt text for all product and styling images.
- Sub-two-second perceived mobile load.
- Lazy-loaded video.
- Static image fallback for every video.
- Clear error states in cart and checkout.
- Concrete delivery dates when possible.

## Overall North Star

Nailismo.com should make men's press-on nails feel stylish, approachable, and culturally current. The final experience should feel like Korean menswear retail entering the nail category: polished, wearable, intimate, and quietly confident.

The product should not feel like a gimmick or a beauty trend borrowed from another audience. It should feel like a new men's styling essential.
