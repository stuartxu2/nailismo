# Nailismo.com Website Design Guideline

## Brand Positioning

Nailismo.com is a press-on nail store built around one feeling: **pure, instant fun**. No niche, no gatekeeping, no "who is this for." Anyone can press on a set and show off in minutes. The brand should feel like opening a fresh pack of candy — bright, playful, a little addictive.

Core positioning:

> Press on. Show off. Nails that are pure fun, ready in minutes.

The product is the toy and the treat at once. The site should make picking a set feel as light and joyful as choosing a flavor, not as serious as a beauty purchase.

## Design Personality

The visual language is inspired by candy packaging, gachapon machines, sticker packs, kawaii character goods, and Y2K toy aesthetics — modernized and clean, not cluttered.

The site should feel:

- Playful
- Bright and sugary
- Bouncy and tactile
- Friendly and approachable
- Gen-Z and TikTok-native
- Joyful, never intimidating
- Collectible, like a toy line

Avoid:

- Muted or "tasteful" minimalism
- Corporate beauty seriousness
- Luxury restraint or quiet-luxury tropes
- Dark, moody, or gothic palettes
- Harsh brutalism or stark mono
- Gendered framing of any kind
- Salon-clinical sterility
- Anything that makes the product feel like a serious grooming decision

## Color Palette

The palette is a candy spread: a soft sugar-pink base with bright, poppy accents used generously and joyfully. Unlike a restrained editorial palette, **color is the identity here** — lean into it.

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Main background | COTTON | `#FFF0F6` | Soft sugar-pink page background |
| Clean surface | CREAM | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, modals, checkout surfaces |
| Primary pop | BUBBLEGUM | `#FF7AC6` | Primary buttons, key accents, brand pink |
| Sky pop | SODA | `#7AE1FF` | Secondary accents, tags, highlights |
| Sunshine pop | LEMON | `#FFE66D` | Badges, stars, playful highlights |
| Mint pop | SPEARMINT | `#9FF2C0` | Success states, fresh accents |
| Grape pop | GRAPE | `#B98CFF` | Tertiary accents, gradients, hover fills |
| Primary text | PLUM INK | `#3A2E39` | Headlines, body text, high-contrast UI |
| Soft neutral | MARSHMALLOW | `#E9D9E4` | Dividers, borders, disabled states |

Recommended usage:

- COTTON / CREAM dominate as surfaces, but the page should never read as "neutral" — accents appear in every viewport.
- Use 2-3 candy pops per section, not all six at once, to stay bright without turning chaotic.
- Soft pastel gradients (BUBBLEGUM → GRAPE, SODA → SPEARMINT) are encouraged for hero areas, buttons, and badges.
- PLUM INK keeps text readable; never use a pop color for long body copy.

Color should feel like an open bag of candy: abundant, sweet, and inviting — but each section still has a clear focal pop so the eye knows where to go.

## Homepage Direction

The homepage should open like a candy display, not a beauty storefront.

The first viewport should burst with color and product: a bright hero with a big bouncy headline, floating nail sets, soft blob shapes, and at least one playful candy gradient. Show many colorful sets immediately so visitors feel the "gotta-collect-them-all" pull.

Recommended hero copy:

```text
Press on. Show off.
Nails that are pure fun — ready in minutes.
```

Primary CTA:

```text
Shop the Candy Rack
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
Find My Size
```

Recommended homepage flow:

1. Big bouncy color-burst hero with floating sets.
2. "New flavors" (new arrivals) in a candy-rack grid.
3. Best sellers / fan favorites.
4. Shop by color (color-blob picker).
5. "Press on in 3 steps" — playful how-it-works.
6. Find My Size — friendly one-tap flow.
7. Creator clips and real customer hand photos.
8. Fast, friendly checkout and shipping reassurance.

## Typography

Typography should be rounded, friendly, and bouncy — sweet but still legible.

Use:

- A rounded, slightly chunky display face for headlines (e.g. Baloo 2, Fredoka, or similar rounded geometric).
- A clean, friendly sans-serif for body and UI (e.g. Nunito or Quicksand).
- Generous size jumps between display and body for playful contrast.
- Sentence case with occasional bold, bouncy emphasis. Light use of playful uppercase on tags only.

Avoid:

- Thin, austere grotesks.
- High-contrast luxury serifs.
- Condensed editorial faces.
- Anything cold, narrow, or corporate.

Example copy style:

- Sugar gloss
- Mint chip
- Bubblegum pop
- Cotton candy
- Soft serve
- Pure fun, zero effort
- Ready in minutes
- Press on. Show off.

## Product Card Design

Product cards should look like candy on a rack: bright, collectible, and fun to tap.

Each card should include:

- Large product image on a soft pop-colored tile.
- Set name (treat-like, e.g. "Mint Chip", "Bubblegum Pop").
- Shape, such as Short Square, Almond, or Oval.
- Finish, such as Matte, Chrome, Sheer, or Gloss.
- Price.
- A row of bright color/finish swatches.
- A bouncy, friendly quick-add button (rounded, candy-colored, with a satisfying press animation).

Use playful labels:

- New flavor
- Almost gone
- Fan favorite
- Back in stock

Cards should have soft rounded corners, a gentle candy-tinted drop shadow, and a little bounce on hover. Avoid loud red discount slashes or heavy borders — keep it sweet, not screaming.

## Product Page Design

Product pages should feel like unwrapping a treat while answering every "wait, how does this work?" question for first-timers.

Every product page should include:

- Macro product photography on a candy-colored backdrop.
- Hand model photos (diverse hands, any gender).
- Nail length comparison.
- Nail shape comparison.
- Fit and sizing guide.
- Application time.
- Expected wear duration.
- Removal instructions.
- What's included in the kit.
- A short vertical product video.
- User-generated hand photos.
- Clear shipping, return, and payment information.

Primary CTA:

```text
Add to Bag
```

Secondary CTA:

```text
Find My Size
```

Product page messaging should focus on ease and joy:

```text
Press on in minutes. Show off all day.
Easy to wear, easy to remove, impossible to resist.
```

## Imagery And Art Direction

Photography and graphics should feel like a candy commercial.

Use:

- Bright, even, poppy lighting.
- Candy-colored seamless backdrops.
- Hands of all kinds, joyfully showing off sets.
- Floating product, confetti, sprinkles, soft blobs, and sticker-style graphics.
- Playful props: gummies, soft toys, bubbles, holographic surfaces.
- Tight, fun crops that celebrate the colors and shapes.

Avoid:

- Muted or moody editorial lighting.
- Beige minimalist flat lays.
- Clinical salon-table imagery.
- Anything dark, serious, or luxury-coded.
- Visual clutter that buries the product.

## Navigation And Layout

Navigation should be simple, friendly, and fun.

Recommended navigation:

- New
- Shop All
- Shop by Color
- Best Sellers
- Fit Guide
- About

Product filters should be playful and practical:

- Color
- Length
- Shape
- Finish
- Vibe (cute, bold, soft, sparkly)
- Beginner-friendly

The layout should use rounded cards, soft pop-colored section bands, blobby dividers, and generous bouncy spacing. Sections can alternate background pops (COTTON, soft SODA, soft LEMON) to keep the page feeling like a candy aisle while staying clean.

## Mobile Experience

The site is mobile-first. Most visitors arrive from TikTok, Instagram, creator links, or search on mobile.

Mobile requirements:

- Fast-loading, colorful hero media.
- Sticky bottom `Add to Bag` bar on product pages.
- One-thumb navigation.
- Swipeable, snappy product carousels.
- Vertical styling videos.
- Simple, friendly sizing flow.
- Guest checkout by default.
- Digital wallet priority.

Checkout should support:

- Apple Pay
- Google Pay
- Shop Pay
- PayPal
- Venmo
- Cash App
- Klarna, Afterpay, or Affirm

Checkout should stay under three steps wherever possible.

## Social Proof And Community

Nailismo should feel like a fun, friendly crowd — everyone's already wearing them.

Include:

- Creator clips.
- Real customer hand photos.
- First-timer reviews ("so easy!").
- "Styled with" fun context.
- Color-of-the-month picks.
- Short application videos.
- Restock request list.
- Early-access signup.

The community layer should feel bubbly and welcoming, never exclusive.

## Launch And Drop Strategy

Drops should feel like new candy flavors hitting the shelf, not hype scarcity.

Recommended launch mechanics:

- Seasonal flavor drops.
- Limited colorways.
- Mystery/blind-box sets.
- Restock request pages.
- "Vote for the next flavor" community picks.
- Early-access for members.
- Soft, fun countdowns.

Collection / set name examples:

- Bubblegum Pop
- Mint Chip
- Cotton Candy
- Soda Pop
- Lemon Drop
- Grape Soda
- Sugar Gloss
- Sprinkles

Keep scarcity light and joyful. Never use fake urgency or fear-based selling.

## Accessibility And Performance

The site should be candy-bright but still fast and usable for everyone.

Requirements:

- WCAG-compliant contrast — verify pop colors against text; use PLUM INK for text, never light pops.
- Clear, friendly focus states (a visible candy-colored ring is encouraged, not removed).
- Keyboard-accessible navigation.
- Descriptive alt text for all product and styling images.
- Sub-two-second perceived mobile load.
- Lazy-loaded video.
- Static image fallback for every video.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — bounce and float animations must calm down when requested.
- Clear, friendly error states in cart and checkout.
- Concrete delivery dates when possible.

## Overall North Star

Nailismo.com should make press-on nails feel like the most fun, low-stakes treat online — bright, collectible, and instant. The experience should feel like a modern candy brand entering the nail category: sweet, playful, welcoming to absolutely everyone, and impossible to scroll past without smiling.

The product should never feel like a serious beauty commitment. It should feel like joy you can press on in minutes.
